to me I thought it was fake internet money for nerds. 

I ignored it for years. Then I watched people turn 

R1000 into R50,000 and I started paying attention.



But I also watched people lose everything chasing hype.



So today I'm giving you the balanced honest truth about

and how to get started safely if you decide it's for you.



\## What Exactly Is Cryptocurrency?



Cryptocurrency is digital money that exists only online. 

Unlike rands or dollars, no government or bank controls 

it. Instead it runs on technology called blockchain — 

a public record of every transaction that nobody can 

fake or delete.



The most famous cryptocurrencies are:



\- Bitcoin (BTC) — the original, digital gold

\- Ethereum (ETH) — powers apps and smart contracts

\- USDC and USDT — stable coins tied to the US dollar

\- Solana (SOL) — fast and cheap transactions



\## Why Do People Invest in Crypto?



Three main reasons:



\- Potential for massive growth — Bitcoin went from 

R1 to over R1 million per coin over 15 years

\- Protection against inflation — limited supply means 

it can't be printed like paper money

\- Access to new financial systems — especially 

powerful for South Africans who want USD exposure



\## The Real Risks Nobody Talks About



I need to be straight with you here because I care 

about your money.



Crypto is extremely volatile. Bitcoin has dropped 

80% in value before — and recovered. But not everyone 

held on through that drop. Many people panic sold 

at the bottom and lost everything.



There are also scams everywhere. If anyone promises 

guaranteed crypto returns or asks you to send crypto 

to receive more back — run. It is always a scam.



\## How Much of Your Money Should Go Into Crypto?



Most financial experts suggest no more than 5% to 10% 

of your investment portfolio in crypto if you choose 

to invest at all. 



Think of it like this. Build your foundation first:



\- Emergency fund of 3 to 6 months expenses

\- TFSA maxed out with ETFs on Easy Equities

\- Then consider crypto with money you can afford to lose



\## How to Buy Crypto Safely in South Africa



The safest way for South Africans is through regulated 

platforms like:



\- Luno — the most popular in SA, very beginner friendly

\- VALR — more advanced, lower fees

\- Easy Crypto SA — buy and hold in your own wallet



Never keep large amounts on an exchange long term. 

Move it to your own wallet once you know what you 

are doing.



\## My Personal Crypto Experience



I put 5% of my portfolio into Bitcoin and Ethereum 

in 2022. I watched it drop by 60%. I did not sell. 

By 2024 it had recovered and then some.



The lesson is not that I am smart. The lesson is 

that crypto rewards patience and punishes panic.



\## Should You Invest in Crypto in 2025?



Only if:



\- You have an emergency fund already

\- You are already investing in ETFs

\- You can afford to lose what you put in

\- You will not check the price every hour



If that sounds like you then yes — a small crypto 

position makes sense in 2025. Start with Bitcoin. 

Keep it simple. Be patient.



If you would stress about losing that money — stick 

to ETFs for now. There is no shame in that. ETFs 

have made more millionaires than crypto ever will.



\## The Bottom Line



Crypto is real. The opportunity is real. But so is 

the risk. Go in with your eyes open, start small, 

and never invest money you cannot afford to lose.



You are already ahead of most people just by 

educating yourself before jumping in. That is 

what a smart girl in finance does.
